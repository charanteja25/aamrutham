Our Story

The Beginning

Born from a boy's love
for mangoes

Ever since childhood, mangoes have been more than just a fruit — they were joy, they were summer, they were family. That love never faded.

"Aam + Amrutham. We believe the perfect mango tastes like pure అమృతం — divine nectar."

With Aamrutham, our goal is to bring you undiscovered, rich-tasting mango varieties you've never tasted before. Every fruit is sourced from our own farms in Bobbili, grown without chemicals, harvested only at peak season.

Our logo tells the whole story: the boy joyfully plucking the mango — that's the founder. The girl eagerly waiting — that's you, anticipating that first divine bite. This is the Aamrutham experience we promise.

Mangoes are laid on dry paddy straw — the method used for generations. They ripen at their own pace, uniformly, with no carbide or chemical agents. The straw breathes with the fruit. The result is sweetness that cannot be faked.

Signature Variety · Bobbili Farms మెట్టవలస పీచు

Mettavalasa
Peechu

— The Fibrous King from the Hills

Named after the elevated village near Bobbili. Cool nights concentrate sugars to extraordinary levels. Thick, silky fibrous strands carry an intense floral sweetness. A mango meant to be sucked, not sliced — a true childhood ritual you never forget.

Brix 20–24 Fibrous Pulp Mettavalasa, Bobbili May – June

Up nextKothapalli Kobbari
Heritage Variety · Same Land, Generations బొబ్బిలి పీచు

Bobbili
Peechu

— The Pride of Bobbili

Born from the red laterite soil of Bobbili, grown on the same land for generations. Rich amber pulp, finer fibres — almost threads of pure flavour. Farmers call it "the mango that tastes like memory." Best squeezed and sipped straight from the skin.

Brix 19–23 Fine-Fibrous Bobbili May – June
✦ Summer 2026
03
Exotic · Nearly Unknown Outside Vizianagaram కొత్తపల్లి కొబ్బరి

Kothapalli
Kobbari

— The Coconut Mango

"Kobbari" means coconut in Telugu — and this variety earns that name completely. Fiberless, cream-textured pulp carries a faint but unmistakable coconut undertone beneath the rich mango sweetness. Originating from Kothapalli village. Nearly unknown outside the district. An experience, not just a fruit.

Brix 21–25 Fiberless Kothapalli, Bobbili June – July
