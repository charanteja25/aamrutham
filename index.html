Farm to Your Doorstep

1

Jeevamrutham Farming

Traditional fermented organic fertiliser — cow dung, herbs, zero chemicals. Nourishing our trees the way nature intended.

2

Pollination & Flowering

Bees and butterflies do their ancient work. We let nature pollinate — the flowers that survive to fruit are the strongest.

3

Fruit Set

Tiny green mangoes form on the branches. We monitor each cluster, thinning where needed so every fruit gets the tree's full nourishment.

4

Hand-Picked at Peak

Farmers examine every mango by hand — the right give, the right colour, the right smell at the stem. No machines. No guessing.

5

Natural Ripening on Straw

Laid on dry paddy straw — the traditional method. Ripen at their own pace, uniformly, with no carbide or chemical agents.

6

Delivered to Hyderabad

Packed with care, dispatched same-day. From our Bobbili farm to your door — so the first bite feels exactly like it should.

Signature · Bobbili Exclusive

Mettavalasa Peechu

మెట్టవలస పీచు — The Fibrous King from the Hills

Named after the elevated village of Mettavalasa near Bobbili, this heritage variety is celebrated for its distinctively fibrous texture — thick, silky strands of pulp that carry an intense, almost floral sweetness. Grown at higher elevations, the cool nights concentrate the sugars to extraordinary levels. A mango that must be sucked, not sliced — a true childhood ritual.

Brix 20–24 Fibrous Pulp Mettavalasa, Bobbili

Signature · Heritage Variety

Bobbili Peechu

బొబ్బిలి పీచు — The Pride of Bobbili

Born from the red laterite soil of Bobbili, this fibrous variety has been grown on the same land for generations. Rich amber pulp with a bold, complex sweetness that lingers long after the last bite. The fibres are finer than other peechu varieties — almost like threads of pure flavour. Local farmers call it the mango that "tastes like memory." Best enjoyed the traditional way: squeezed and sipped straight from the skin.

Brix 19–23 Fine-Fibrous Bobbili

Exotic · Aamrutham Pick

Kothapalli Kobbari

కొత్తపల్లి కొబ్బరి — The Coconut Mango

One of the most unusual mangoes you'll ever taste. "Kobbari" means coconut in Telugu — and this variety earns that name completely. Its flesh carries a faint but unmistakable coconut undertone layered beneath a rich mango sweetness. Originating from Kothapalli village, it is nearly unknown outside the Vizianagaram district. Fiberless, cream-textured pulp that melts on the tongue. An experience, not just a fruit.

Brix 21–25 Fiberless Kothapalli, Bobbili

Royal · Dessert Mango

Imam Pasand

ఇమామ్ పసంద్ — Favourite of the Imam · Also: Himayat

Known as Himayat in Andhra, this is the royal mango — said to have been the Imam's favourite, hence the name. Extraordinarily large, with a deep saffron-hued pulp that is smooth, thick, and almost creamy. Zero fibre. The sweetness is profound yet balanced — never cloying. A single fruit can weigh up to a kilogram. Grown for centuries in the Deccan region, it is now rare and seasonal, available only for a few weeks at peak summer.

Brix 18–22 Fiberless · Creamy Bobbili
